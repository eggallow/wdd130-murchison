Songs

#1. Iris

- The Goo Goo Dolls


I heard this song from my dad, and it instantly caught my attention. Iris doesn't follow the traditional song structure we're all used to but rather a big, gradual emotional climb to the final cinematic feeling chorus that slams like no one's business. This song is actually where I got the idea for what I'd like to name my first daughter - Iris.

#2. Dreams

- Fleetwood


I remember this girl I met in middle school that I always had a crush on. I remember she moved far away but came back for a couple years. I asked her out when she came back and introduced me to this band, Fleetwood Mac. Fleetwood Mac is probably my favorite band now. Not because of her, but because of the way it makes me feel. The bittersweet harmony in all their best songs make me feel a little sad, very reflective, but also motivated all at the same time. Dreams is the epitome of this feeling for me.

#3. #41

- Dave Matthew Band


If I could give a theme song to my Winter 2026 semester in college, it's this one, without a doubt. Funny thing is, though, as much as I love this song and emotionally connect to it, the songwriter Dave Matthews even admits in interviews that he's got no idea what the songs about at all. Instead of being about lyrics, #41 is about pure musical emotion and movement. The unclear lyrics actually help in this case, you get to choose and therefore it becomes your song. You gotta hear this to really know what I mean by all this. This time of year has been the most difficult for me emotionally, and this song embodies those struggles to a perfect t.

#4. 4EVER

- Clairo


This song was my jam in high school. I think Clairo was one of my favorite artists at the time, and she still is way up there. What makes her so good? Rather than being the kind of artist that stands and performs at you, she feels more like she's sitting next to you, thinking out loud. She's not begging for your attention, she's doing her own thing. 4EVER feels like your floating and where other songs are expanding and grab your attention, Clairo gets you with introspective softness.

#5. Satellite

- Dave Matthew Band


The Dave Matthews Band is something else - they got two songs on my top ten. Satellite makes me think about the girl I'll marry one day. She feels far away and mysterious. I think a lot about who she'll be like and how I'll meet her. I don't know, but Satellite tells me she's there and that the wait will be worth it.

#6. Purple Rain

- Prince & The Revolution


This song felt was the song for me in the very end of high school. Prince actually did explain some of the meaning and themes behind the song. Purple Rain, essentially, is a moment in your life of emotional and spiritual crisis, when everything changes or falls apart. High School was ending and my two-year mission in Guatemala was about to start. Everyone I knew from home would be gone. I knew after graduation night, I wasn't going to see most of them again. Did I have the relationships with those people that I wanted? Did I make the most of my time? I felt like I didn't and that was hard for me. That being said, Purple Rain is about change. I'm changing, I'm going somewhere new and so the only thing I can do is look ahead.

#7. Ex Factor

- Lauryn Hill


Ex-Factor is a recent one for me too. Apple music actually ranks this whole album as the number one greatest album of all time. No, I don't agree with them, but this song has so much feeling in it. She definitely has talent and this song is unbelievably catchy.

#8. Nutshell

- Alice in Chains


This is one of those main character songs when everything seems sad and down, which is definitely what Alice in Chains was going for. My aunt actually introduced me to this band, so shoutout to her.

#9. End of the Road

- Boyz II Men


CooleyHighHarmony is a great description of this entire album and actually everything Boyz II Men has put out. These guys are too good at harmonizing, too good at it. End of the Road is probably there most famous song, and with good reason. I can't get enough of their style and their charm. Play this song as a last resort to fix a relationship.

#10. Sideways

- Santana & Citizen Cope


Sideways without Santana is alright, a listen-once song at best. With Santana's legendary guitar, it's suddenly a perfect mix of cool, slightly hazy, introspective. Me and my cousin jammed to this in a very memorable trip in college. Lots and lots of nostalgia points.
